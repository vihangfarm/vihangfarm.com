Organic Farming

As the world awakens to the ill effects of excessive use of synthetic chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and insecticides, monoculture, and water-intensive farming practices, a sizeable population has been working towards organic farming practices before it was a worldwide movement. Vihang is an attempt to contribute towards this movement, by promoting natural and organic farming practices. This is achieved with the use of natural manure and insecticides, heirloom seeds, water harvesting techniques, mixed cropping, trap crops, etc.

Sustainable Living

Our planet has gifted us with finite resources which are available to fulfill our worldly needs. However, with an infinite demand and consumerist mindset, there is mounting pressure on the planet's available resources. Vihang works towards sustainability by measuring and managing carbon footprint via choices of food, energy, and water consumption. Vihang also contributes to ecological balance by undertaking initiatives such as heirloom seed conservation, promotion of traditional housing, construction of climatic structures, and awareness programs.

Exploitation-free Farming

India has been projected as an agrarian economy. On the other hand, India faces an agrarian crisis as more farmers are withdrawn from the fields with each passing day. This exodus is primarily driven by mass exploitation of human and environmental rights. Vihang works towards curtailing exploitation by fetching fair price for produce, local partnerships and equitable distribution of wealth within locals, and by actively running programs for ecological balance. Vihang has been able to raise an active customer base that is committed to exploitation-free consumption.

about us

Prachi and Kirti belong to a family that lost their farmland in one of the hydro-electric projects initiated by the Government in 1993. Vivid memories of Sunday visits at the farm, and of grand parties which followed every sorghum and wheat harvest lingered on while they both grew up to become professionals.


Two decades later, in year 2013, they decided to buy a small piece of land to relive their passion. Prachi, an IT professional, and Kirti, a lecturer at the time, quit their jobs and started practising sustainable farming and experimenting with ecological restoration. They named their farm ‘Vihang’, which is a Marathi word for bird.


What started for Prachi with a course on ecology with the Ecological Society, Pune, and for Kirti with Masanobu Fukuoka’s “One Straw Revolution”, Vihang has now grown into an initiative that spans across organic farming practices, sustainable living, and exploitation-free farming. During this journey, Vihang has received inspiration, support and guidance from eminent advocates of poison-free farming Mr Vasant Futane and Mr Manohar Parchure. The nationwide movement for organic farming initiated by Padmashree Subhash Palekar has also been an inspiration for Vihang.

Initiatives

Workshops

Regular workshops on water harvesting, soil conservation, preparation of organic fertilizers, insecticides, and pesticides.

Nature Trails

A walk in the adjacent forest, visit to the sacred grove, bird-watching programs, tree walks, insect walks, stargazing.

Family Farmer

Family Farmer, is a unique program wherein consumers can visit the farm and get to know the farmer by working alongside in the fields.

Marketing

Supply and distribution of fresh organic produce directly to consumers. Revenue thus generated is equitably shared with local farmers.
